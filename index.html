Your Security Is Our Priority

We built Vaulter with privacy and security as the foundation.

Local-First Architecture

Vaulter stores all your passwords locally on your device, encrypted with your master password. Your data never touches our servers unless you explicitly choose to sync between your own devices.

Zero-Knowledge Encryption

We use AES-256 encryption, the same standard used by banks and governments. Your master password is never stored or transmitted - only you can decrypt your data.

Open Source Transparency

Vaulter's code is open for security experts to audit. We believe in transparency so you can verify our security claims yourself.

// AES-256 Encryption Implementation
const
crypto = require('crypto');

function encrypt(text, password) {
  const salt = crypto.randomBytes(16);
  const key = crypto.pbkdf2Sync(password, salt, 100000, 32, 'sha256');
  const iv = crypto.randomBytes(16);
  const cipher = crypto.createCipheriv('aes-256-cbc', key, iv);
  let encrypted = cipher.update(text);
  encrypted = Buffer.concat([encrypted, cipher.final()]);
  return salt.toString('hex') + ':' + iv.toString('hex') + ':' + encrypted.toString('hex');
}

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Vaulter.

Vaulter takes a local-first approach to password management. Unlike most password managers that store your data in the cloud, Vaulter keeps everything encrypted on your own device. This means your passwords never leave your control and aren't vulnerable to cloud-based breaches. You get the convenience of a password manager without the risk of third-party storage.

Vaulter offers optional end-to-end encrypted backup to your other devices. This means you can choose to sync your encrypted passwords between your own computers and phones without ever storing them on our servers. If you don't set up sync, we recommend keeping a backup of your encrypted vault file in a secure location. Your master password is the key to decrypt your data, so make sure to remember it or store it securely.

Vaulter uses industry-standard AES-256 encryption to protect your data locally. Your passwords are encrypted before being stored, and can only be decrypted with your master password. Even if someone gains access to your device, they won't be able to access your passwords without your master password. We also implement additional security measures like memory wiping to prevent password leaks.
